What is P2Pool?

P2Pool is a decentralized mining pool that operates without a central server. Instead of trusting a pool operator with your hashrate and funds, P2Pool uses its own sidechain to coordinate miners and distribute rewards directly to your wallet.

Originally forked from SChernykh's P2Pool for Monero, this implementation has been extensively rewritten and improved for Salvium.

Features

Decentralized
No central server that can be shutdown or blocked. No pool admin to go rogue.
0% Fee
All rewards go directly to miners. No pool fees, no payout fees.
Trustless
No pool wallet. Funds are never in custody. Blocks pay directly to miners.
PPLNS Payouts
Fair reward distribution based on shares in a 6-hour sliding window.
Uncle Blocks
Orphaned shares are still counted. All your work is accounted for.
Fast Blocks
10-second sidechain block time for frequent share submissions.

How Payouts Work

When you mine on P2Pool, you submit shares to the sidechain. Each share stays in the PPLNS window for up to 2160 blocks (~6 hours).

When P2Pool finds a Salvium mainchain block, the reward is split proportionally among all miners with shares in the PPLNS window. Your payout is based on the total difficulty of your shares relative to the window's total difficulty.

Note: If your shares exit the PPLNS window before a block is found, they won't receive a payout. However, over time this averages out - some shares may receive multiple payouts when blocks are found quickly.

Requirements

  • Salvium Node: A fully synced salviumd v1.0.7 or newer (download)
  • P2Pool: Latest version from GitHub releases

P2Pool Mini & Nano

Coming Soon: The P2Pool Mini and Nano sidechains are not yet operating for Salvium. These lower-difficulty sidechains will be launched once there is sufficient hashrate and demand. For now, please use the main P2Pool sidechain.

When available, miners with lower hashrate will be able to use these sidechains to find shares more frequently:

  • Mini: Lower difficulty for smaller miners. Use --mini flag (port 38888)
  • Nano: Even lower difficulty for very small miners. Use --nano flag

Troubleshooting

Check P2Pool Logs

grep -E 'WARNING|ERROR' p2pool.log

Common Issues

  • No shares found: Wait for salviumd and P2Pool to fully sync. Check that ports are open.
  • Connection refused: Ensure salviumd is running with --zmq-pub option.
  • Low hashrate: Make sure huge pages are enabled. Check CPU temperature.
